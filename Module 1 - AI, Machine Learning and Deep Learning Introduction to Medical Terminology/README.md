## AI, Machine Learning and Deep Learning Introduction to Medical Terminology
Medical terminology is the foundation of every AI application built for healthcare. A model trained to detect disease, flag risk, or support clinical decisions is only as useful as its ability to work within the language of medicine. This module addressed that foundation directly. The problem was straightforward: without fluency in clinical vocabulary, engaging meaningfully with AI in healthcare is not possible. This assignment required researching ten medical terms drawn from current clinical literature, understanding what each term means, why it matters, and how it appears in real research contexts.

The approach was rooted in primary and peer-reviewed sources. Each term was pulled from recent publications in journals like the New England Journal of Medicine and paired with definitions from authoritative sources like the National Cancer Institute and Radiopaedia. The terms spanned a wide range of conditions and treatments, from delayed cerebral ischemia following subarachnoid hemorrhage to monoclonal antibodies like fremanezumab and efgartigimod, to imaging findings like ground-glass opacities. Each entry included a definition, a direct citation from a clinical study showing the term in context, and an explanation of its significance to patient outcomes.

Working through these terms made clear how much clinical specificity matters in healthcare AI. A term like "hemolytic" carries different weight depending on whether it appears in a diagnostic model, a treatment protocol, or a prognostic tool. Understanding that distinction is what separates a technically functional model from one that is clinically meaningful. This module established the vocabulary needed to engage seriously with every topic that followed in the course.
